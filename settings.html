Automation & autonomy

How much the pipeline does on its own — from filling repeat prices to sending finished quotes. Start conservative; loosen once you trust it.

Auto-fill repeat lines
Off — the sales team picks each first-time match
When a line is an exact repeat of one your team already resolved before, the pipeline reuses that decision automatically instead of flagging it again. When off, every unmatched line waits for a human — exactly as today.
The send gate
Send green-tier quotes automatically
Off — every quote waits for you
The one money-facing switch. When on, only quotes that clear both green gates below send on their own. Amber and red always wait. When off, nothing sends — the pipeline just drafts.
Green · minimum confidence 90/100

How sure the match has to be before a quote counts as green. Higher = the system only trusts itself on near-certain matches.

Green · minimum margin 20%

The deal also has to be healthy. Below this margin a quote can never be green — it drops to amber for a look.

Amber · minimum confidence 60/100

The floor for "worth reviewing." Above this (but short of green) a quote is amber. Below it, the quote is red — flagged for a careful look.

Thin-margin floor 15%

Any quote landing under this margin is tagged needs approval — held for a human even when confidence is high. Your safety net against underpricing.
