The product

From "fiyat talebi" to a finished draft in five passes.

You keep your mailbox, your price files, and the final word. Quotewright does the reading, the matching, and the arithmetic in between — then waits for you.

Pass 01

Reads the inbox

Every incoming email is classified. RFQs are picked out; newsletters, invoices, and noise are ignored without a trace.

Pass 02

Reads the whole thread

In any language. Quantity in a second email, the size in a third? It assembles the full request across every message — like a good salesperson would.

Pass 03

Matches your catalog

Weight, size, colour, packaging — matched against your own product master, kept current straight from your SAP / ERP.

Pass 04

Prices both ways

Cash and term prices from your list — or the customer's own contract price — per m², per pack, per box, with today's central-bank FX applied.

Pass 05

Waits for you

A branded draft with a PDF attached lands in your mailbox and a summary lands on your phone. Approve to send, or keep editing. You are the gate.

The pricing rule

Your prices are yours.

Quotewright will happily do the reading and the arithmetic all day. But it will never make up a number — not from cost, not from margin, not from a hunch.

Only catalog values

Every price in every quote traces to a cell in your own price file — or to that customer's agreed contract price. If it's not on record, it's not in the quote.

What it can't confirm, it flags

When a request is ambiguous — a size that rounds, a colour that needs a sample — it prices the closest catalog match and flags the point for you, rather than guessing silently.

You are the send button

Every quote waits for a human approval before it reaches a customer. On your phone, in one tap.

Everything in the box

Nine things it does that a template can't.

Never invents a price

No estimating, no "cost plus margin," no filling gaps with plausible numbers. Every price traces to your own price file. Anything uncataloged is named and routed to your sales team.

uncataloged line → routed, never faked

Reads any language

Turkish, English, German, Bulgarian, French — a request comes in one language and the quote goes back in it, with colour names, units and trade terms translated in both directions.

"navy blue" → lacivert · "2'li paket" → 2 pcs/pack

Reads the whole thread

Follow-ups, corrections and specs scattered across several emails are merged into one understood request — and any second company copied on the thread is set aside, not mixed in.

3 messages → one understood order

Every line answered

Each requested product gets its own section: priced, pending your customer's missing detail, or pending your own pricing. The quote ends with a single "information we still need" list.

5 items asked → 5 sections answered

Cash & term, side by side

Both payment options on every line, with the term days your list defines — 30, 60, 90 — and local-currency equivalents at today's central-bank rate.

Cash $3.11/m² · Term 90d $3.44/m²

Per-customer contract pricing

When a customer has agreed prices on record, those win over the list price — matched safely on name and account code, and never disclosed to anyone else.

agreed price on file → applied automatically

Branded email + PDF

Each draft is a clean, on-brand HTML email with a matching PDF quotation attached — headers, line table, badges, totals and your address, ready to send as-is.

HTML reply + attached PDF quote

Knows an accepted offer

When a customer replies to accept, it recognises the order and issues a final confirming quotation — restating the agreed lines and totals and promising a proforma invoice.

"we accept" → confirming quotation + proforma

Catalog synced from SAP

Your product master, packaging and price columns sync straight from SAP or your ERP, so every quote is built on today's catalog — not last quarter's spreadsheet.

SAP / ERP → always-current catalog

Plus a private quote console: every draft it produces is logged, searchable, and tracked from draft to won or lost.
