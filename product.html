The product

From "fiyat talebi" to a finished draft in five passes.

You keep your mailbox, your price files, and the final word. Quotewright does the reading, the matching, and the arithmetic in between — then waits for you.

Pass 01

Reads the inbox

Every incoming email is classified. RFQs are picked out; newsletters, invoices, and noise are ignored without a trace.

Pass 02

Reads the whole thread

Customer sent the quantity in a second email? The size in a third? It assembles the full request across every message — like a good salesperson would.

Pass 03

Matches your catalog

Weight, size, colour, packaging — matched against your own product master, loaded from the cost files you already keep.

Pass 04

Prices both ways

Cash and term prices from your list — per m², per pack, per box, line totals — with today's central bank FX rates applied.

Pass 05

Waits for you

The draft lands in your mailbox and a summary lands on your phone. Approve to send, or keep editing. You are the gate.

What a finished quote contains

Complete enough to send. Honest enough to trust.

The pricing rule

Your prices are yours.

Quotewright will happily do the reading and the arithmetic all day. But it will never make up a number — not from cost, not from margin, not from a hunch.

Only catalog values

Every price in every quote traces to a cell in your own price file. If it's not in the file, it's not in the quote.

What it can't confirm, it flags

When a request is ambiguous — a size that rounds, a colour that needs a sample — it prices the closest catalog match and flags the point for you, rather than guessing silently.

You are the send button

Every quote waits for a human approval before it reaches a customer. On your phone, in one tap.

Everything in the box

Six things it does that a template can't.

Every line answered

Each requested product gets its own numbered section: priced, pending your customer's missing detail, or pending your own pricing. The quote ends with a single "information we still need" list.

5 items asked → 5 sections answered

Cash & term, side by side

Both payment options on every line, with the term days your list defines — 30, 60, 90 — and local-currency equivalents at today's central bank rate.

Cash $3.11/m² · Term 90d $3.44/m²

Straight from your catalog

No estimating, no "cost plus margin," no filling gaps with plausible numbers. Every price on every line traces to a cell in your own price file — cash and term, in the right currency for each product division.

USD for microfibre · EUR for felt — each from your list

One-tap approval

Each quote pings your phone with the totals and the full text. Approve to send the reply from your mailbox; decline to keep it as an editable draft. Nothing sends itself.

Totals + full text → approve / keep as draft

Answers in their language

A Turkish request gets a Turkish quote; an English request gets an English one — colour names, units, and trade terms translated correctly in both directions.

"navy blue" → lacivert · "2'li paket" → 2 pcs/pack
