The product

From "fiyat talebi" to a finished draft, in five passes.

You keep your mailbox, your price files, and the final word. Quotewright does the reading, the matching, and the arithmetic in between — then waits for you to approve it.

Pass 01

Reads the inbox

Every incoming email is classified. Real RFQs are picked out; newsletters, invoices, and noise are ignored without a trace — including a reply that simply accepts an earlier offer.

Pass 02

Reads the whole thread

In any language. Quantity in a second email, the size in a third? It assembles the full request across every message — and sets aside any other company copied in — like a good salesperson would.

Pass 03

Matches your catalogue

Weight, size, colour, packaging — matched against your own product master, kept current straight from your SAP or ERP. The exact SKU, or the closest with a note.

Pass 04

Prices both ways

Cash and term prices from your list — or the customer's own contract price when one is on record — per m², per pack, per box, with today's central-bank FX applied.

Pass 05

Waits for you

A branded draft with a PDF quote attached lands in your mailbox, and a one-line summary lands in the console. Approve to send, or edit first. You are the gate.

The pricing rule

Your prices are yours.

Quotewright will happily do the reading and the arithmetic all day. But it will never make up a number — not from cost, not from margin, not from a hunch.

Only catalogue values
Straight from your file

Every price in every quote traces to a cell in your own price file — or to that customer's agreed contract price. If it isn't on record, it isn't in the quote.

Flagged, not faked
What it can't confirm, it flags

When a request is ambiguous — a size that rounds, a weight that's close — it prices the nearest catalogue match and flags the point for you, rather than guessing silently.

Human gate
You are the send button

Every quote waits for a human to approve it before it reaches a customer. Draft-first, always — nothing leaves on its own.

Needs-you queue

Only what wants a human

A short, ranked list of drafts and questions that actually need your eyes — ambiguities, big orders, uncatalogued lines. Everything else stays out of your way.

One-tap actions

Resolve without leaving the row

Approve & send, reply, clarify a missing spec, or relabel a misfiled email — each an inline action, so a queue of ten clears in a couple of minutes.

Customer memory

Remembers who it's talking to

Preferred products, past quantities, agreed contract prices and quirks are kept per customer, so a returning request arrives with context already attached.

Learning loop

Repeat requests self-resolve

Corrections you make once inform the next draft. Common, well-understood requests start coming through cleaner — needing a glance, not a rebuild.

Catalogue gaps

Every hole worth filling

Each item routed to sales for want of a price is logged as a gap. Fill the recurring ones in your catalogue and those requests start pricing themselves.

Insights & activity

The numbers behind the queue

Win rate, quote volume and margin trends, plus a full activity feed — every draft tracked from produced, to approved, to won or lost.

Automation settings

You decide how much it does on its own.

Start at draft-only, where every quote waits for you. As trust builds, dial it up — auto-classify, auto-draft, even auto-send inside guardrails you define, per customer and per value. The gate never disappears; you choose where it sits.
